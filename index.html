Overview

Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) Main is a national-level entrance exam conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA). It serves as a gateway to top engineering colleges in India, including NITs, IIITs, and other Centrally Funded Technical Institutions (CFTIs). It is also the qualifying exam for JEE Advanced, which is required for admission into the prestigious Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs).

The exam is held twice a year, in January and April. Candidates can appear in one or both sessions. The best score out of the two attempts is considered for admissions.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Qualification: Candidates must have passed 10+2 or equivalent examination with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.
  • Age Limit: There is no age limit for appearing in JEE Mains.
  • Year of Passing: Candidates must have passed Class 12 in the last two years or be appearing in the current year.
  • Attempts: A candidate can attempt JEE Mains for three consecutive years. Each year has two sessions.

Exam Pattern

JEE Mains consists of two papers: Paper 1 (for B.E./B.Tech) and Paper 2 (for B.Arch and B.Planning).

Paper 1 (B.E./B.Tech)

  • Mode: Computer-Based Test
  • Duration: 3 Hours
  • Subjects: Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics
  • Questions: 90 (30 per subject; 20 MCQ + 10 Numerical, only 5 numerical to be answered)
  • Marking: +4 for correct, -1 for incorrect (MCQs only)

Syllabus

The syllabus is based on the NCERT curriculum of Classes 11 and 12.

Physics

  • Kinematics
  • Laws of Motion
  • Work, Energy and Power
  • Electrostatics
  • Magnetism
  • Current Electricity
  • Optics and Modern Physics

Chemistry

  • Atomic Structure
  • Chemical Bonding
  • Thermodynamics
  • Organic Chemistry (Hydrocarbons, Alcohols, Ketones, etc.)
  • Coordination Compounds

Mathematics

  • Algebra
  • Calculus
  • Trigonometry
  • Probability
  • Coordinate Geometry

Preparation Tips

  1. Start your preparation with NCERT books. Build strong conceptual understanding.
  2. Make a daily and weekly timetable. Prioritize tough topics.
  3. Solve previous years' question papers and take mock tests.
  4. Use online platforms like NTA Abhyas app for practice.
  5. Stay consistent and focused. Avoid burnout.

Recommended Books

  • Physics: HC Verma, DC Pandey Series, NCERT
  • Chemistry: OP Tandon, Morrison & Boyd (Organic), NCERT
  • Maths: RD Sharma, Cengage Series, Arihant Series

Important Dates (Tentative)

  • Application Start: November
  • Session 1 Exam: January
  • Session 2 Exam: April
  • Result Declaration: Within 3-4 weeks of exam
